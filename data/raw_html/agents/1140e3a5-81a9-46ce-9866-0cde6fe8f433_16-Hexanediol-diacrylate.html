1,6-Hexanediol diacrylate

Agent Name
1,6-Hexanediol diacrylate
Alternative Name
HDDA
CAS Number
13048-33-4
Formula
C12-H18-O4
Major Category
Plastics & Rubber
Synonyms
HDDA; 1,6-Hexamethylene diacrylate; 1,6-Hexanediol di-2-propenoate; 1,6-Hexanediol diacrylate; 2-Propenoic acid, 1,6-hexanediyl ester; Acrylic acid, hexamethylene ester; C 716; Hexamethylene acrylate; Hexamethylene diacrylate; Hexamethylene glycol diacrylate; Hexaneglycol diarcylate; Kayarad HDDA; NK Ester A HD; Photomer 4017; SR 238; Sartomer 238; Sartomer SR 238; Setalux UV 2243; Viscoat 230; 2-Propenoic acid, 1,1'-(1,6-hexanediyl) ester; [ChemIDplus] HDODA; [AIHA] 1,6-HDDA; 1,6HX-A; HMDA; [IUCLID]
Category
(Meth)acrylates
Description
Clear yellow liquid; [CAMEO]
Sources/Uses
Chemical intermediate and curing agent for polymers; [HSDB] Used as a cross-linking agent in UV-cured inks, coatings, adhesives, and dental sealants; [AIHA] Used for synthesis in the chemical industry, in paints, lacquers, and varnishes, in photochemicals and the photographic industry, and as intermediate; [IUCLID]
Comments
Allergic contact dermatitis reported in a laboratory worker in a paint manufacturing factory; [Kanerva, p. 1742] Can undergo exothermic polymerization reactions; Temperature sensitive; May auto-oxidize on exposure to air forming explosive peroxides; [CAMEO] Classified in the EU as a skin and eye irritant with the possibility of causing skin sensitization; [IUCLID] May spontaneously polymerize; A skin and strong eye irritant; May cause skin sensitization; [Alfa Aesar MSDS]

Diseases, Processes, and Activities Linked to This Agent

Diseases

Occupational diseases associated with exposure to this agent:

  • Contact dermatitis, allergic
Processes

Industrial Processes with risk of exposure:

  • Painting (Pigments, Binders, and Biocides)
  • Photographic Processing
  • Plastic Composites Manufacturing
  • Silk-Screen Printing
  • Textiles (Printing, Dyeing, or Finishing)
  • Working with Glues and Adhesives
