Spray epoxy or polyurethane paint, shellac, lacquer, or varnish

Job Task Name
Spray epoxy or polyurethane paint, shellac, lacquer, or varnish
Comments
Occupational asthma (OA) has been reported in workers exposed to anhydrides, isocyanates (TDI, MDI, HDI, IPDI, hydrogenated MDI), triglycidyl isocyanurate (TGIC) and aliphatic and cycloaliphatic amines (N,N-Dimethyl-1,3-propanediamine, ethylenediamine, triethylene tetramine, dimethylethanolamine, isophorone diamine, and hexamethylenetetramine). [Malo] Hypersensitivity pneumonitis (HP) may be caused by isocyanates and trimellitic anhydride. For isocyanate HP, the antigens are the isocyanate chemicals, e.g., TDI, MDI or HDI; the exposure occurs during the production or use of polyurethane paints, resins, or foams; [Harber, p. 202] An asthma death was reported in 2003 after spraying a polyurethane truck-bed liner containing MDI; [PMID: 15940723]
